summary_en: "In the 2014 World Cup final, where Germany beat Argentina 1–0, German goalkeeper Manuel Neuer collided with Argentina's Gonzalo Higuaín near the edge of the box in the 56th minute; referee Nicola Rizzoli reportedly awarded no penalty and no card. Argentine commentators believed a penalty should have been given, with newspaper Clarín lamenting the denied appeal, while a La Nación writer considered the referee correct not to award it. The decision remains debated and was not officially overturned."
