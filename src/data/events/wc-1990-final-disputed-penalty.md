summary_en: "In the 1990 World Cup final between Argentina and West Germany, Mexican referee Edgardo Codesal awarded West Germany a penalty in the 85th minute for a foul by Roberto Sensini on Rudi Völler. The decision was widely debated, with Argentine players protesting furiously. Andreas Brehme converted the spot kick for a 1-0 West German win. Reports noted Codesal had earlier refused multiple penalty appeals from both sides, including stronger German claims, making the late decision especially contentious. Maradona blamed the referee for the loss."
